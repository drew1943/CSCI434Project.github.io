Introduction

The Rubik's Cube is a classic puzzle that can look overwhelming at first, but it becomes very manageable once you learn a beginner-friendly method. This walkthrough explains the basic steps for solving a cube, and includes visuals and a video to help you understand the process.

What You Need

  • A standard 3x3 Rubik’s Cube
  • Good lighting (helps with color recognition)
  • Patience during the first few attempts

Step-by-Step Beginner Method

1. Solve the White Cross

Goal: Make a white cross on one face while aligning the side colors with the center pieces of the adjacent faces.

How to do it: Use intuitive moves to bring white edge pieces to the bottom (or top) layer depending on how you hold the cube for orientation. Focus on matching both the white face and the side colors with their centers. This step usually doesn’t require strict algorithms, just careful observation.

2. Solve the White Corners

Goal: Complete the first layer by placing the four white corner pieces in their correct positions and orientations.

Algorithm: R' D' R D

Instructions: Hold the solved white cross on the bottom. Place an unsolved white corner in the bottom-front-right slot with the white sticker on the side or top. Apply R' D' R D repeatedly until the white sticker is on the bottom. Repeat for all four corners.

3. Solve the Middle Layer Edges

Goal: Insert the four edge pieces into the middle layer without disturbing the solved white face.

Algorithms:

Edge to the right: U R U' R' U' F' U F

Edge to the left: U' L' U L U F U' F'

Instructions: Identify the edge in the top layer. Align it with the center of the corresponding color. Decide if it goes to the left or right and use the matching algorithm. Repeat for all middle layer edges.

4. Form the Yellow Cross

Goal: Form a yellow cross on the top face regardless of corner positions.

Algorithm: F R U R' U' F'

Instructions: Depending on your top face pattern, hold the cube accordingly and apply the algorithm until the yellow cross forms.

5. Orient the Yellow Edges

Goal: Rotate the yellow edge pieces so they match the adjacent center colors.

Algorithm: R U R' U R U2 R' U (used as needed for each edge)

Instructions: Identify misoriented edges and apply the algorithm until all edges are aligned correctly.

6. Position Yellow Corners

Goal: Place all yellow corners in their correct location (orientation may not be correct yet).

Algorithm: U R U' L' U R' U' L

Instructions: Keep a correctly placed corner at the front-right of the top face. Apply the algorithm until all corners are in their correct positions.

7. Orient Yellow Corners

Goal: Rotate yellow corners so the top face is completely yellow.

Algorithm: R' D' R D (applied repeatedly per corner)

Instructions: Hold the corner to rotate at the front-right of the top layer. Apply the algorithm until the yellow sticker is on top. Turn the top layer to the next corner and repeat.

8. Solve the Final Layer Edges

Goal: Swap the remaining top edges to complete the cube.

Algorithm (Edge Swap): F2 U L R' F2 L' R U F2

Apply until all edges are in place. After this, the Rubik's Cube will be completely solved!

Final Tips

The more you practice the steps, the faster you will become at recognizing patterns and moving smoothly through the solution. Beginners often take ten to fifteen minutes per solve, and that time improves naturally with repetition.

Please take a look at the link page for some helpful sites if you are curious about speed solving or other twisty puzzles.

Beginner Method Algorithms: Practice each algorithm slowly until it becomes familiar.

  • White Corners: R' D' R D
  • Middle Layer Edges Right: U R U' R' U' F' U F
  • Middle Layer Edges Left: U' L' U L U F U' F'
  • Yellow Cross: F R U R' U' F'
  • Orient Yellow Edges: R U R' U R U2 R' U
  • Position Yellow Corners: U R U' L' U R' U' L
  • Orient Yellow Corners: R' D' R D
  • Final Layer Edge Swap: F2 U L R' F2 L' R U F2
